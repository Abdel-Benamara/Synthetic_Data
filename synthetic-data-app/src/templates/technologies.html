CTGAN

CTGAN is a GAN-based method to model tabular data distribution and sample rows from the distribution. In CTGAN, the mode-specific normalization overcome the non-Gaussian and multimodal distribution. A conditional generator and training-by-sampling were design to deal with the imbalanced discrete columns. And fully-connected networks and several recent techniques to train a high-quality model are used.

TVAE

Variational autoencoder is another neural network generative model. VAE was adapted to tabular data by using the same preprocessing and modifying the loss function. This model is called TVAE. In TVAE, two neural networks are used to model coefficients, and they are trained using evidence lower-bound (ELBO) loss.

WGAN

Wasserstein GAN, or WGAN, is a type of generative adversarial network that minimizes an approximation of the Earth-Mover's distance (EM) rather than the Jensen-Shannon divergence as in the original GAN formulation. It leads to more stable training than original GANs with less evidence of mode collapse.
